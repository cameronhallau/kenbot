Submission checklist

Article settings

- Word count between 450 and 550, not including tables and in-article disclosures.
- Generated disclosure.
- Removed unnecessary tickers.

Article copy

- Good title with curiosity, FOMO, and hook factor, but not clickbait.
- Excerpt expands on the title without repeating it.
- Keyword appears in title, excerpt, and first sentence.
- First mention of all listed company names in bold.
- Do not excessively mention the company name, use "the company" or other similar placeholders once it has been introduced to avoid repetition.
- Added ticker for companies with at least 125 words of analysis, for example `(LSE:AZN)`.
- Stock price chart of the primary ticker, for example `[fool_stock_chart ticker=LSE:AZN]`.
- Added two relevant internal links, not in the introduction or conclusion.
- Article is balanced, highlighting both risks and potential rewards.
- All questions are answered, particularly the headline.
- Jargon-free and easy to read for beginners.
- No recommendation made.

Article element settings

- Tables: turn off fixed cell width.
- Lists: leave a single line gap after the end of a list.
- Remove all white space after the end of the article.

House style

Punctuation

- Crossheads such as H2 and H3 should be sentence case.
- Each bullet should end with a full stop.
- Brand names should be italicised.
- Do not add dots to UK or US.

Numbers

- Write out numbers from one to nine, excluding titles.
- Use digits for 10 and above.
- Remove decimal places if the digit is zero.

Quotations

- Quotes from businesses or people should be italicised and use double quotation marks.
- Words in air quotes should not be italicised and should use single quotation marks.

Names

- Use first name and last name only on first mention.
- Use last name only thereafter.

Hyphens

- Use a short dash "-" for connecting words.
- Use a long dash "–" with spaces on both sides when injecting or connecting clauses.
- Only hyphenate compound words when they are leading.

Commas

- Use commas to separate every element in a sentence list.

Dates

- Use the `D Month Year`, `D Month`, or `Month` formats.

Currency

- Use symbols when discussing currency amounts.
- Million: `£1m`.
- Billion: `£1bn`.
- Trillion: `£1trn`.

Niche instances

- Use `forecast`, not `forecasted`.
- Use `pricey`, not `pricy`.
- Use `focused`, not `focussed`.
- A crash is when something falls by at least 20% in a short space of time.
- A correction is when something falls by at least 10% gradually over time.

Navigating regulations

- Do not include a direct call to action on a particular investment such as buy, sell, or hold.
- Encourage the reader only to `consider`, `think about`, `mull over`, and similar soft phrasing.
- Do not say another investor would buy a particular stock unless it is clearly framed as opinion.
- Do not include the word `you` in relation to a specific stock in a title or body copy.
- Do not promise or imply a guaranteed outcome.
- Use phrases such as `aim to`, `strive for`, or `target` instead of certainty.
- Do not discuss cryptocurrencies.
- Always relate the discussion to the writer's own portfolio or investors in general, never to an individual.
- Always ensure the article is balanced with both company-specific risks and company-specific potential rewards.
